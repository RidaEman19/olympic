EILEEN GU

biography

Eileen Gu is an Olympic champion freestyle skier, global role model, and fashion icon - all of which she achieved before turning 20. At the Olympic Winter Games Beijing 2022, she made history as the first freestyle skier to win three medals at a single Games: gold in big air and halfpipe and silver in slopestyle. 18 years old at the time, she became the youngest Olympic freestyle skiing champion and the first woman to land a forward double cork 1440, solidifying her reputation as a "triple-threat" across the disciplines of halfpipe, big air, and slopestyle. In addition to her Olympic triumphs, this freestyle ski prodigy is a two-time world champion, three-time Winter X Games champion, and two-time Winter Youth Olympic Games gold medalist. Born on 3 September, 2003, in San Francisco, California, to an American father and a Chinese mother, Gu embraced her multicultural upbringing. She began skiing at the age of three, balancing her weekend trips to the slopes with academics and pursuing long-distance running. At just nine years old, Gu became the champion of the junior group in the USA Snowboard and Freeski Association, advancing to the senior ranks by age 13.
