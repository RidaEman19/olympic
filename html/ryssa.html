RYSSA LEAL

biography

At the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games in July 2021, a beaming 13-year-old with a youthful face and braces proudly bites on a silver medal. Rayssa Leal's infectious joy and unbridled enthusiasm shone through after becoming one of the youngest Olympic medallists in history. "It’s like I’m in an amusement park and I’m just having fun," she told Olympics.com, reflecting on her joie de vivre and winning mentality. "Because I am doing what I like and what was my dream to always do. So just getting to championships and being with my family and with my friends is great," she explained.


Born in Brazil in 2008, Leal entered the global spotlight through the power of viral videos and social media. A short clip of seven-year-old Leal flawlessly executing a heelflip down a set of stairs while wearing a bright blue tutu as part of a fairy princess costume captivated audiences across social media platforms. Skateboarding megastar Tony Hawk joined the social media frenzy, praising the young prodigy and elevating her online fame.
